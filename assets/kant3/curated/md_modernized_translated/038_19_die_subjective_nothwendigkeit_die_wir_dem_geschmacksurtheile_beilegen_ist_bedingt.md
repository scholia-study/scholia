## § 19. The subjective necessity that we ascribe to the judgment of taste is conditioned

The judgment of taste expects assent from everyone; and whoever declares something to be beautiful wills that everyone ***ought*** to give approval to the object before him and likewise declare it to be beautiful. The ***ought*** in the aesthetic judgment is thus, even according to all the data that are required for judging, pronounced only conditionally. One solicits the assent of everyone else because one has a ground for it that is common to all; on which assent one could also count, if only one were always certain that the case were correctly subsumed under that ground as {{ 64 }} the rule of approval.
