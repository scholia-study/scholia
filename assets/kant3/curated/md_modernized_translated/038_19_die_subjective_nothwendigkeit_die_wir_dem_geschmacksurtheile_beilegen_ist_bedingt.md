## § 19. The subjective necessity that we ascribe to the judgment of taste is conditioned

Assent is what the judgment of taste asks of everyone; and anyone who pronounces something beautiful wills that everyone ***should*** grant the object in question their approval and likewise pronounce it beautiful. Hence the ***ought*** in the aesthetic judgment is, even in view of all the data required for the judging, still pronounced only conditionally. One woos every other person's assent because one holds a ground for it shared by all; and on that assent one might well reckon, were one but always certain that the case were rightly subsumed beneath that ground, taken as {{ 64 }} the rule of approval.
