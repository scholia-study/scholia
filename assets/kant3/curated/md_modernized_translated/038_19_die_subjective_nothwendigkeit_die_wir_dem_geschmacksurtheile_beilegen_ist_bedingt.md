## § 19. The subjective necessity that we ascribe to the judgment of taste is conditioned

The judgment of taste demands the agreement of everyone; and whoever declares something to be beautiful wants that everyone ***should*** give their approval to the object at hand and likewise declare it beautiful. Hence the ***ought*** in the aesthetic judgment is, even in view of all the data required for the judging, still pronounced only conditionally. One solicits the agreement of everyone else because one has a ground for it that is common to all; and one could indeed count on such agreement, if only one were always certain that the case had been correctly subsumed under that ground as {{ 64 }} the rule of approval.
