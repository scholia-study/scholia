## {{{ 247 }}} {{ 79 }} § 24. On the division of an investigation of the feeling of the sublime

As concerns how the moments of the aesthetic judging of objects fall to be divided in their bearing on the feeling of the sublime, the analytic is able to run its course according to the same principle as was followed in the dissection of judgments of taste. For the satisfaction in the sublime, precisely like that in the beautiful, is a verdict of the aesthetic reflecting power of judgment, and as such it must exhibit itself as universally valid as regards ***quantity***, as devoid of interest as regards ***quality***, as subjective purposiveness as regards ***relation***, and, as regards ***modality***, must present the latter as necessary. Herein, then, the method will not diverge from that of the previous section: unless indeed one were to count it for something that we began, in the former case, where the aesthetic judgment had to do with the form of the object, by examining quality; but that here, in the case of the formlessness that can pertain to what we name sublime, we will make our beginning with quantity, the first among the moments of the aesthetic judgment on the sublime: the ground for this, however, is to be gathered from the preceding §.

But the analysis of the sublime requires a division which that of the beautiful can do without, namely one into the ***mathematically-sublime*** and into the ***dynamically-sublime***.

For inasmuch as the feeling of the sublime has for its character a ***movement*** of the mind joined to the judging of the {{ 80 }} object, while taste for the beautiful presupposes and maintains the mind in ***calm*** contemplation; and inasmuch as this movement is nonetheless meant to be judged as subjectively purposive (the sublime, after all, pleases): it is by the imagination brought into relation either with the ***faculty of cognition*** or with the ***faculty of desire***, though in either relation the purposiveness of the given representation is to be judged merely with regard to these ***faculties*** (without end or interest): whereupon the object receives the first as a ***mathematical*** and the second as a ***dynamical*** attunement of the imagination, and is therefore represented as sublime in the said twofold fashion.
