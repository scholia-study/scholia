## {{{ 247 }}} {{ 79 }} § 24. On the division of an investigation of the feeling of the sublime

As concerns the division of the moments of the aesthetic judging of objects in relation to the feeling of the sublime, the Analytic will be able to proceed according to the same principle as was done in the analysis of judgments of taste. For as a judgment of the aesthetic reflecting power of judgment, the satisfaction in the sublime must, just as well as that in the beautiful, be represented as universally valid according to ***quantity***, as without interest according to ***quality***, as subjective purposiveness according to ***relation***, and the latter as necessary according to ***modality***. In this, then, the method will not deviate from that in the previous section: unless one were to count it as something that there, where the aesthetic judgment concerned the form of the object, we began with the investigation of quality; whereas here, given the formlessness that can belong to that which we call sublime, we will begin with quantity, as the first moment of the aesthetic judgment about the sublime: the ground for which, however, is to be seen from the preceding §.

But the analysis of the sublime requires a division that the analysis of the beautiful does not need, namely that into the ***mathematically*** and the ***dynamically sublime***.

For since the feeling of the sublime carries with it, as its character, a ***movement*** of the mind connected with the judging of the {{ 80 }} object, whereas the taste for the beautiful presupposes and maintains the mind in ***restful*** contemplation; but this movement is to be judged as subjectively purposive (because the sublime pleases): it is related by the imagination either to the ***faculty of cognition*** or to the ***faculty of desire***, but in both kinds of relation the purposiveness of the given representation is to be judged only with respect to these ***faculties*** (without an end or interest): for then the first is ascribed to the object as a ***mathematical***, the second as a ***dynamical*** disposition of the imagination, and therefore the object is represented as sublime in the said twofold way.
