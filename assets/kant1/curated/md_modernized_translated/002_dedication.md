{{{ 3 }}} To His Excellency, the Royal State Minister, Baron von Zedlitz.

## {{{ 5 }}} {{ V }} Gracious Sir!

To advance the growth of the sciences, for one's own part, is to labor in Your Excellency's own interest; for this interest is most intimately bound to the sciences not merely through the exalted office of a patron, but through the far more familiar relationship of a devotee and enlightened connoisseur. For this reason I also avail myself of the sole means that in some measure lies within my power, to attest my gratitude for the gracious trust that Your Excellency is pleased to place in me, as though I might contribute something toward this aim.

{{ VI }} To that same gracious regard with which Your Excellency deemed the first edition of this work worthy, I now dedicate this second one also, and with it at once all the remaining affairs of my literary vocation, and remain, with the deepest veneration,

Your Excellency's most submissive and obedient servant, Immanuel Kant. Königsberg, 23 April 1787.
