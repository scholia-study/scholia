{{{ 3 }}} His Excellency, the Royal Minister of State, Baron von Zedlitz.

## {{{ 5 }}} {{ V }} Gracious Sir!

To promote the growth of the sciences for one's own part is to work in Your Excellency's own interest; for this is most intimately connected with them not merely through the exalted post of a patron, but through the much more familiar relationship of a lover and enlightened connoisseur. Therefore, I avail myself of the only means that is in a certain measure within my power, to show my gratitude for the gracious trust with which Your Excellency honors me, as though I could contribute something to this end.

{{ VI }} To the same gracious attention with which Your Excellency honored the first edition of this work, I now also dedicate this second edition, and therewith at the same time all other concerns of my literary vocation, and am, with the deepest reverence,

Your Excellency's most humble and obedient servant, Immanuel Kant. Königsberg, the 23rd of April 1787.
