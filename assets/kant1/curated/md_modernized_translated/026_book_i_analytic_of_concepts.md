## First Book. Analytic of Concepts

By the analytic of concepts I do not understand the analysis of them, or the usual procedure in philosophical investigations, {{{ 84 }}} of dissecting concepts that present themselves according to their content and bringing them to distinctness, but rather the hitherto little attempted ***dissection of the faculty of understanding itself***, in order to investigate the possibility of concepts _a priori_ by seeking them out in the understanding alone, as their birthplace, and analyzing its pure use in general; {{ 91 }} for this is the peculiar business of a transcendental philosophy; the rest is the logical treatment of concepts in philosophy in general. We shall therefore follow the pure concepts to their first seeds and predispositions in the human understanding, in which they lie prepared, until they are finally developed on the occasion of experience, and, freed by the very same understanding from the empirical conditions attaching to them, are exhibited in their purity.
