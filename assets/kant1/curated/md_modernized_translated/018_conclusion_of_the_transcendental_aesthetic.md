## {{{ 73 }}} {{ 73 }} Conclusion of the Transcendental Aesthetic

Here we now have one of the required pieces for the solution of the general problem of transcendental philosophy: ***how are synthetic propositions _a priori_ possible?***, namely pure intuitions _a priori_, space and time, in which we, when we wish to go beyond the given concept in an _a priori_ judgment, encounter that which can be discovered _a priori_ not in the concept, but rather in the intuition that corresponds to it, and which can be synthetically connected with the former, which judgments, however, for this reason can never extend further than to objects of the senses and can only be valid for objects of possible experience.
