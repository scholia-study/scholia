## {{{ 73 }}} {{ 73 }} Conclusion of the Transcendental Aesthetic

Here we now have one of the requisite pieces for the resolution of the general task of transcendental philosophy: ***how are synthetic propositions possible _a priori_?***, namely pure intuitions _a priori_, space and time, in which we, whenever in an _a priori_ judgment we wish to pass beyond the given concept, come upon what can be discovered _a priori_ not in the concept but indeed in the intuition that corresponds to it, and can be connected synthetically with the former, which judgments, however, for this reason never reach further than to objects of the senses and can be valid only for objects of a possible experience.
