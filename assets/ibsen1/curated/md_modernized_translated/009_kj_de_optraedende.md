## CHARACTERS:

- Emperor Julian.
- Nevita the Commander.
- Potamon the goldsmith.
- Caesarius of Nazianzus the Royal Physician.
- Themistius the orator.
- Mamertinus the orator.
- Ursulus the treasurer.
- Eunapius the barber.
- Barbara the woman.
- Hecebolius the scripture-scholar.
- Courtiers and officers of state.
- Townsfolk of Constantinople.
- Envoys from the kings of the East.
- Eutherius the steward.
- Servants in the palace.
- Judges,
- Medon the corn-merchant.
- Malchus the tax-collector.
- Gregory of Nazianzus, *Caesarius's brother.*
- Phocion the dyer.
- Publia the woman.
- Her son Hilarion.
- Agathon of Cappadocia.
- Bishop Maris of Chalcedon.
- Agathon's younger brother.
- The procession of Christian prisoners.
- Heracleus the poet.
- Oribases the Royal Physician.
- Libanius the orator, *city-governor in Antioch.*
- Apollinaris the hymn-writer.
- Cyril the teacher.
- An old priest at Cybele's sanctuary.
- The women hymn-singers in Antioch.
- Fromentinus the captain.
- Jovian the Commander.
- Maximus the mystic.
- Numa the soothsayer.
- Two other Etruscan soothsayers.
- Prince Hormisdas, *a Persian in exile.*
- Anatolus the captain of the body-guard.
- Priscus the teacher of wisdom.
- Cytron the teacher of wisdom.
- Ammian the captain.
- Basil of Caesarea.
- His sister Macrina.
- A Persian deserter.
- Roman and Greek soldiers.
- Persian warriors.

@stage (The first act takes place in Constantinople, the second act in Antioch, the third act likewise in Antioch, the fourth act in and around the eastern regions of the empire, and the fifth act on the plains beyond the Tigris. The action spans the period from the month of December in the year 361 to the end of June in the year 363.)
