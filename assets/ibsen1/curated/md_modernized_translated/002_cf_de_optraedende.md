## CHARACTERS:

- Emperor Constantius.
- Empress Eusebia.
- Princess Helena, *the emperor's sister.*
- Prince Gallus, *the emperor's cousin.*
- Prince Julian, *Gallus's younger half-brother.*
- Memnon, *an Ethiopian, the emperor's body-slave.*
- Potamon the goldsmith.
- Phocion the dyer.
- Eunapius the barber.
- A fruit-seller.
- A captain of the watch.
- A soldier.
- A painted woman.
- A palsied man.
- A blind beggar.
- Agathon, *a vinedresser's son from Cappadocia.*
- Libanius the teacher of wisdom.
- Gregory of Nazianzus.
- Basil of Caesarea.
- Sallust of Perusia.
- Hecebolius the scripture-scholar.
- Maximus the mystic.
- Eutherius the steward.
- Leontes the quaestor.
- Myrrha the slave-girl.
- Decentius the tribune.
- Sintula the master of the horse.
- Florentius the general.
- Severus the general.
- Oribases the physician.
- Laipso the under-officer.
- Varro the under-officer.
- Maurus the standard-bearer.
- Soldiers, churchgoers, heathen onlookers, courtiers, priests, students at the schools of wisdom, dancing-girls, servants, the quaestor's retinue, Gallic warriors.
- Visions and voices.

@stage (The first act takes place in Constantinople, the second act in Athens, the third act in Ephesus, the fourth act at Lutetia in Gaul, and the fifth act at Vienna in the same country. The time spans the interval between the year 351 and the year 361.)
